Syracuse was once the most powerful city in the Greek world. Today its heart is Ortigia — a small island of honey-coloured stone, baroque churches, and market stalls selling swordfish and blood oranges. The Mediterranean light here has a particular quality — sharp, golden, almost theatrical.

Sicily's east coast rewards those who slow down. Morning espresso in Piazza Duomo, afternoon swim at Fontane Bianche, evening passeggiata along the waterfront. Syracuse is ancient history living at human pace — exactly what tutto passa means.
