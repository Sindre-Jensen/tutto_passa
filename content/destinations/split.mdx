Split is a city built inside a Roman emperor's retirement palace. Two thousand years later, laundry hangs from palace windows, espresso bars occupy ancient cellars, and ferries depart hourly for some of the Mediterranean's most beautiful islands.

The Adriatic here is impossibly clear. Mornings belong to the green market and harbour coffee; afternoons to swimming and island planning; evenings to konoba dinners and Riva strolls. Split is Croatia's most lived-in coastal city — and its most useful base.
