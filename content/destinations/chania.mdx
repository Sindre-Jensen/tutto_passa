Chania is what people picture when they dream of Greece — whitewashed buildings, a lighthouse at the harbour mouth, tables spilling onto cobblestones, and the scent of grilled sardines in warm air. Crete's second city wears its Venetian and Ottoman past with ease.

Life here moves to Cretan rhythm. Long lunches, afternoon swims, evening volta (promenade walks), and raki toasts with strangers who become friends. Chania is the Mediterranean lifestyle distilled — sun, sea, food, and nowhere to be.
