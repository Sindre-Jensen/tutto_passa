Porto is Portugal unfiltered — gritty, golden, and deeply proud. The Douro carves through the city, port wine cellars line the Gaia waterfront, and every surface seems covered in blue and white tiles. It's slower than Lisbon, cheaper, and in many ways more intimate.

The lifestyle here is river walks, long lunches, and evenings that start with a glass of tawny port as the sun drops behind the Dom Luís bridge. Porto doesn't try to impress — it simply is.
