Lisbon sits where the Tagus meets the Atlantic, a city built on seven hills and centuries of maritime ambition. Today it feels more relaxed than rushed — locals linger over coffee, evening walks along the river are a ritual, and the light turns everything gold by late afternoon.

The city wears its history lightly. Tiles cover entire buildings, trams rattle up impossibly steep streets, and every neighbourhood has its own rhythm. For anyone chasing the Mediterranean lifestyle without leaving Western Europe, Lisbon delivers sunshine, surf nearby, and a cost of living that still makes sense.
